### 10. Impact Crouch & Footfall
*Live — read per-frame. Variables: `impactCrouchFactor`, `crouchDepthRatio`, `crouchDissipationRate`, `impactSquashOvershoot`, `squashPunchDecayRate`, `landingCaptureWindow`, `footfallImpulse`, `footfallMinSpeed`*

**Architecture note — Impact Crouch**: When a foot locks from airborne, `FootMovement` captures the maximum downward speed across both feet over a `landingCaptureWindow` window (to handle two-footed landings where the feet don't lock simultaneously). `PlayerSkeletonRoot` then converts that speed to crouch depth: `crouchAmount = min(landingSpeed × impactCrouchFactor, standHeight × crouchDepthRatio)`. The cap means heavier landings still clamp — `crouchDepthRatio` sets how much energy the body can store. Crouch dissipates at `crouchDissipationRate` pixels/second unless the player holds down, which freezes it indefinitely. The stored `crouchAmount` feeds the jump formula directly: `jumpMagnitude = jumpSpeed + (hipOffset + crouchAmount × pixelToWorld) × jumpOffsetFactor` — so a frozen crouch is a jump loaded and ready. On landing, a separate `squashPunch` is added on top of the visual crouch: `visualCrouch = crouchAmount + squashPunch`. The punch decays at `squashPunchDecayRate`, independently of the freeze, so the visual snap is always immediate even when the gameplay crouch is held.

**Architecture note — Footfall**: Each time a foot transitions from Stepping to Locked, `FootMovement` applies a forward impulse to the torso: `force = footfallImpulse × clamp01(1 − |vel.x| / maxSpeed) × sign(vel.x)`, but only when `|vel.x|` is between `footfallMinSpeed` and `maxSpeed`. The speed-scaling means the impulse is strongest at low-to-mid speed and fades to zero at the sprint cap — it accelerates you into a run without pushing a full-sprint past its limit.

| Variable | Low value effect | High value effect |
|---|---|---|
| `impactCrouchFactor` (0.3 → 1.0) | Even hard landings produce little crouch — system barely readable | Small falls produce deep crouch — large crouch from any landing |
| `crouchDepthRatio` (0.2 → 0.6) | Shallow cap — heavy landings don't store much energy; jump bonus is modest | Deep cap — hard landings load a big jump bonus; compression is visually dramatic |
| `crouchDissipationRate` (8 → 30 px/s) | Crouch lingers a long time — forgiving window to cash the jump bonus | Crouch bleeds off fast — must jump immediately after landing to use it |
| `impactSquashOvershoot` (1.0 → 2.0) | No extra visual punch — landing compression matches the gameplay crouch exactly | Strong visual slap on landing — body compresses well past the stored crouch, then snaps back |
| `squashPunchDecayRate` (25 → 60 px/s) | Slow visual snap-back — punch lingers and overlaps with dissipation | Instant snap — the landing punch reads as a hit, not a settle |
| `landingCaptureWindow` (0.03 → 0.08 s) | Very tight window — second foot must land almost simultaneously to register | Wider window — two-footed landings reliably capture the full impact speed |
| `footfallImpulse` (0.1 → 1.0) | Barely perceptible forward kick per step — walking and running feel identical in acceleration | Strong kick each step — running has a visible ratcheting quality; starting to run feels punchy |
| `footfallMinSpeed` (0.3 → 1.0) | Impulse fires even at a crawl — stationary pivoting can trigger it | Impulse only fires during committed movement — no boost from slow shuffles |

**What to observe — Impact Crouch**: Drop from a high platform onto flat ground. Watch the body compress on landing: the squash punch should read as a distinct visual hit, separate from the slower gameplay crouch beneath it. Hold down immediately after landing — the body should stay low. Then release and jump — the jump should be noticeably higher than a flat-ground jump. Now try landing without holding down and jumping in the `crouchDissipationRate` window: this is the "bounce jump" skill expression. Low dissipation rate = forgiving bounce window; high = snap-decision timing.

**What to observe — Footfall**: Walk across flat ground, then sprint. The footfall impulse should make acceleration feel grounded and physical — each step pushes you forward slightly, so running gathers momentum over steps rather than feeling like a smooth analogue slide. At max speed the impulse should be near-silent (scaled to zero), so it never causes runaway. The `footfallMinSpeed` threshold should feel like a dead zone: slow pivoting doesn't trigger a kick, but committing to a direction does.

**The loaded jump**: The design intent is that a deep crouch — either from a big fall or held from a shallower one — is visibly readable as stored energy. The body posture should communicate "ready to explode" before the jump fires. `crouchDepthRatio` controls how dramatic that posture can get; `crouchDissipationRate` controls how long it stays readable. If the window is too short, the skill expression collapses: players can't learn to time it. If too long, the system feels passive rather than intentional.

**Key interaction — Dim 4 (Step Shape)**: Footfall impulse fires once per completed step. Step cadence (controlled by Dim 4's `baseStepDuration` and `stepSpeedScale`) determines how often the impulse fires. A fast cadence at sprint speed means many small kicks; slow step arcs mean fewer, more spaced-out boosts. Tune footfall impulse strength alongside step cadence — a fast-stepping character at low `footfallImpulse` can feel similar to a slow-stepping character at higher impulse.

**Key interaction — Dim 6 (Hip Spring)**: `hipDampingRatio` controls how fast the hip spring recovers after a landing. A low damping ratio leaves the hip oscillating below the locked foot Y for longer — this extends the `jumpOffsetFactor` bonus window in Dim 9, but it also means the visible crouch posture (which is driven by actual hip displacement) lingers longer independently of `crouchDissipationRate`. The two systems stack: a high `crouchAmount` pushes the hip further down on jump, and a slow hip recovery keeps the bonus open after the crouch itself has dissipated.

**Key interaction — Dim 9 (Jump Feel)**: `jumpOffsetFactor` reads `crouchAmount` directly as part of `totalHipCompression`. These two dimensions are tightly coupled: `crouchDepthRatio` sets the ceiling on how much energy this dimension can contribute to jump height, and `jumpOffsetFactor` sets how aggressively that energy converts to air. A high `jumpOffsetFactor` with a deep `crouchDepthRatio` means a fully loaded bounce jump can dramatically outperform a flat-ground jump — which is the skill ceiling. Tune the ratio between them by comparing a held-crouch jump to a flat-ground jump: the bonus should feel like a reward, not a requirement.

**Log when**: Landing from height produces a visible body compression that reads as absorbed impact. Holding down keeps the character visibly loaded. Releasing into a jump gives a noticeably higher arc than a flat-ground jump — rewarding but not overwhelming. The footfall impulse makes acceleration feel physical without causing speed to ratchet uncontrollably at max velocity.
